The Market

Anilao is a rare kind of destination

On the Verde Island Passage — the "centre of the centre" of global marine biodiversity — Anilao sits three hours from a metro of 13 million people, yet ranks among the planet's top macro-diving destinations. The demand is real; the digital storytelling is where it's won or lost.

What it tells us

Intent clusters into three journeys

Destination intent — "anilao diving", "anilao dive resort" — travellers choosing where to go. This is the highest-value ground and the most competitive.

Certification intent — "PADI course Philippines" — new divers ready to spend on a course, often first-timers with no resort loyalty yet.

Niche intent — "macro diving", "underwater photography" — smaller but rising, high-margin, and perfectly matched to Anilao's signature. A wedge worth owning early.

Finding 02 · Audience

Four divers worth speaking to

Anilao doesn't have one audience — it has several, each finding Casa Escondida through a different channel and deciding on different signals.

📸
International · High Value

The Macro Photographer

Travels the world for rare critters. Books multi-day, off-peak stays and spends on guiding, Nitrox and repeat trips.

  • Follows Instagram & YouTube dive creators
  • Won by species galleries & guide skill
  • Plans months ahead
🚗
Domestic · High Volume

The Weekend Manila Diver

Drives down Friday night, dives Saturday–Sunday. Price- and convenience-sensitive, loyal once impressed.

  • Lives in Facebook dive groups
  • Won by packages, transfers & reviews
  • Books days, not months, ahead
🤿
Domestic + Intl · Entry

The New Diver

Searching "learn to dive" or "PADI Open Water". First certification, no resort loyalty — the acquisition moment.

  • Found via Google & AI answers
  • Won by clear course info & trust
  • High lifetime value if converted
👥
Groups · Reliability

The Trip Organiser

Books for dive clubs, camera groups or corporate teams. Needs responsiveness, capacity and predictable logistics.

  • Reached via referrals & DM
  • Won by fast replies & group rates
  • One booking = many divers

Finding 04 · Content & Channels

The reef is the content

Underwater short-form video is the single most under-used asset in the Anilao market. A flamboyant cuttlefish or a hairy octopus stops the scroll in a way no beach photo can — and Casa Escondida sits on top of that footage every diving day.

Meanwhile, review platforms and AI assistants increasingly decide the shortlist before a human ever visits a website. Both reward consistency and structure — exactly what a managed programme delivers.

The thesis

Build an owned engine, not rented reach

Two-thirds of the effort goes into content and discoverability — assets that keep working after they're made. Paid social is the accelerant, not the engine, and a light-touch review system quietly compounds trust in the background.

It's a strategy Anilao's crowded market has largely overlooked — which is exactly why it's Casa Escondida's opening.

The Bottom Line

The demand exists. The lane is open.

Anilao's divers are already searching, scrolling and asking AI where to go. The market is crowded but generic — no single resort has claimed the macro-photography story, the short-form video feed, or the AI-search answer. Casa Escondida has the credentials, the location and the raw footage to own all three. This showcase is how we'd do it.
